DATA TRANSPARENCY & TRACKING

COOKIE PREFERENCES

Effective Date: June 20, 2026. This policy explicitly details how The Keyframe Awards Network uses cookies and browser data tracking mechanisms to run our high-performance streaming and voting panels.

1. What are Cookies and Tracking Tokens?

Cookies and web storage assets are tiny data packets placed on your terminal browser when you explore websites. On our network, we keep tracking to a absolute bare minimum, using these assets purely to maintain smooth UI states and prevent security manipulation across our global voting brackets.

2. Strictly Necessary Security Cookies

These core operational trackers are mandatory to maintain platform stability. They manage your current active interface session and, most importantly, process your anonymous, unique device-locked tokens during the voting window. These prevent script bots or automated repeat-clickers from ruining the leaderboards. Disabling these cookies through your personal browser settings will lock you out from voting eligibility.

3. Performance & Streaming Session Cache

When you tune in to watch our creator showcases or broadcast updates, our system uses local session memory to manage network traffic routing. These assets monitor local bandwidth speeds to help deliver the highest possible bitrate from our stream relays, while saving you data by caching static code elements locally on your laptop or phone.

4. Third-Party Media Cookies

Because the platform links out directly to distribution channels on external corporate networks (such as TikTok Live and YouTube Live), interacting with those components may trigger background telemetry operated by Google LLC or ByteDance Ltd. We do not manage, read, or have access to the advertising or behavioral cookies deployed by those third-party networks.

5. Managing Your Preferences

You maintain absolute structural control over your data. You can clear, block, or completely disable cookies through your personal browser configuration menus at any time. Keep in mind that doing so will erase your cached interface preferences and reset your network connectivity status on our streaming grids.
